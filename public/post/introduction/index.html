Rotating points in a circle

I’ll begin to describe the surprisingly interesting musical experiment which first piqued my interest into how, from certain simple musical problems, unexpected patterns and relationships can emerge and be, partially, explained through mathematics.

The musical experiment is simple:

  1. Pick any circle.
  2. Choose a finite amount of points contained in that circle.
  3. Rotate each of those points, along the chosen circle, by the same amount and in equal direction (clockwise or anticlowise).
  4. We’ll discuss this later in the current post.

Visual examples

From a musical perspective, we’ll be dealing with chords (collections of more than two notes) and their transpositions (translating them by some distance), but, from a mathematical perspective, we only need to follow the already mentioned steps 1, 2 and 3.

We’ll work with the unitary circle, the circle with radius 1 and whose center is the origin of the Cartesian Plane. Each point in the unitary circle can be thought of as a possible musical note, if we conveniently match them.
